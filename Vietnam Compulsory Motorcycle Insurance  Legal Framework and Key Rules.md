# Vietnam Compulsory Motorcycle Insurance: Legal Framework and Key Rules

## Executive Summary

Compulsory motorcycle insurance in Vietnam is regulated under the general regime of compulsory civil liability insurance of motor vehicle users.
Motorcycle owners must purchase and carry a valid policy that covers civil liability for bodily injury, death, and property damage caused to third parties and passengers when the vehicle is used in traffic.[^1][^2]
The current framework is mainly based on Decree 03/2021/ND-CP, as updated by Decree 67/2023/ND-CP, and detailed by Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC, together with traffic-sanctions decrees on fines for not carrying valid insurance.[^3][^4][^1]

## 1. Main Legal Instruments

- **Decree 03/2021/ND-CP** on compulsory civil liability insurance of motor vehicle users (effective from March 1, 2021), replacing Decree 103/2008/ND-CP.[^5][^1]
- **Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC** of the Ministry of Finance guiding a number of articles of Decree 03/2021/ND-CP, including premium schedules and claim procedures.[^6][^4]
- **Decree 67/2023/ND-CP** on compulsory civil liability insurance of motor vehicle owners, fire and explosion insurance, and compulsory insurance for construction investment activities, which adjusts liability limits and premiums but keeps core mechanisms from Decree 03/2021/ND-CP.[^7][^3]
- **Decree 100/2019/ND-CP**, as amended by **Decree 123/2021/ND-CP**, and later **Decree 168/2024/ND-CP**, which define administrative fines for not having or not carrying compulsory insurance when operating motorcycles in traffic.[^8][^9]

## 2. Scope of Application

### 2.1 Vehicles and persons covered

The compulsory regime applies to all "motor vehicle users" operating within the territory of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam, including owners of motorcycles, motorbikes, mopeds (including electric mopeds), three-wheeled motorcycles and similar vehicles under the Road Traffic Law.[^3][^1]
Motor vehicle users include both the legal owner and any person authorized by the owner to lawfully possess or operate the motorcycle.[^1]

### 2.2 Compulsory nature

Owning a motorcycle that participates in traffic in Vietnam requires purchasing compulsory civil liability insurance; this is a legal condition for the vehicle to be allowed on public roads.[^10][^1]
Each motor vehicle is issued **only one** insurance certificate for this compulsory coverage, and the policy must be in force when the vehicle is used in traffic.[^11][^12]

## 3. Types of Motorcycle Insurance

Vietnamese practice distinguishes between compulsory civil liability insurance and various voluntary add-on products.[^10]
For motorcycles, the only **mandatory** product is **compulsory civil liability insurance of motor vehicle users** (often called "motorcycle insurance" or "third-party liability" insurance); voluntary products (such as own-damage, theft, or higher limits) are optional.[^2][^10]

## 4. Key Contract Parameters

### 4.1 Insurance period

For two-wheeled motorcycles, three‑wheeled motorcycles and mopeds (including electric mopeds), the compulsory insurance period is from **1 year up to a maximum of 3 years**.[^13][^11]
Shorter periods (under 1 year) are only allowed in special cases such as foreign vehicles temporarily imported for re-export or vehicles with temporarily registered plates.[^3]

### 4.2 Geographic scope

The insurance applies to accidents caused by insured motor vehicles participating and operating in traffic **within the territory of Vietnam**.[^1][^3]

### 4.3 Form of certificate (paper and electronic)

Insurers must issue an insurance certificate that records vehicle identification, insured parties, liability limits, term, and insurer details.[^1]
Under Decree 03/2021/ND-CP and guiding documents, the certificate may be in paper or electronic form, provided it can be presented to authorities during inspection; many insurers now provide e-certificates via mobile apps or emails in addition to paper cards.[^4][^1]

## 5. Mandatory Coverage

### 5.1 Risks covered

Compulsory motorcycle insurance covers **non‑contractual civil liability** of the motor vehicle user for:

- Damage to health or life of third parties caused by the motorcycle in traffic.
- Damage to property of third parties caused by the motorcycle.
- Damage to health or life of passengers carried on the insured motor vehicle.[^2][^1]

The coverage is limited to accidents where the motorcycle participates in road traffic and operates within Vietnamese territory.[^1]

### 5.2 Who receives compensation

In case of an accident within the scope of insurance, the insurer pays the insured (motor vehicle user) or directly pays the accident victims, their heirs or representatives, for legally recognized damage, subject to liability limits and degree of fault.[^14][^5]
If the insured dies or lacks legal capacity, payment can be made directly to victims, their heirs, or legal representatives.[^5]

## 6. Liability Limits (Sums Insured)

### 6.1 Current limits under Decree 67/2023/ND-CP

Decree 67/2023/ND-CP sets unified **maximum coverage limits** applicable to all compulsory civil liability insurance policies, including for motorcycles:[^7][^3]

- **Health and life damage:** up to **150 million VND per person per accident**, regardless of vehicle type.[^7][^3]
- **Property damage caused by motorcycles, motorized tricycles, mopeds (including electric mopeds) and similar vehicles:** up to **50 million VND per accident**.[^15][^3][^7]

Insurers are not obliged to pay amounts exceeding these statutory maximum limits, unless the vehicle owner has purchased additional voluntary insurance.[^3]

### 6.2 Relation to Decree 03/2021/ND-CP and Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC

Decree 03/2021/ND-CP and Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC previously provided detailed liability schedules by type of damage (for example, tables of compensation by type and degree of bodily injury).[^15][^4]
Decree 67/2023/ND-CP harmonizes and reaffirms these limits (150 million VND per person for health and life; 50 million VND per accident for property for motorcycles), and insurers must apply these ceilings when calculating indemnity.[^16][^7][^3]

## 7. Premiums for Compulsory Motorcycle Insurance

### 7.1 Standard annual premiums

Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC provides Annex I with basic annual premiums for compulsory civil liability insurance by motor vehicle type.[^17][^6]
For motorcycles and similar vehicles, the core premium levels (excluding 10% VAT) are:[^6][^7]

| Vehicle type | Engine / classification | Annual premium (VND, excluding VAT) |
|--------------|-------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Two‑wheeled motorcycle | Up to 50 cc | 55,000 |
| Two‑wheeled motorcycle | Over 50 cc | 60,000 |
| Three‑wheeled motorcycle | – | 290,000 |
| Electric moped / e‑scooter | Electric moped | 55,000 |
| Other scooter‑type / similar vehicles | – | 290,000 |

These premium levels are confirmed in later guidance and Q&A on compulsory motorcycle insurance pricing through 2023–2024.[^6][^7]

### 7.2 Premium adjustments and short-term policies

Decree 03/2021/ND-CP allows insurers to **increase premiums up to 15%** above the standard rate based on the vehicle’s accident history and the insurer’s risk tolerance.[^14]
For policies with duration other than one year (for example, certain short-term covers), premiums are prorated by days based on the standard annual premium, according to a formula set out in Article 9 of Decree 03/2021/ND-CP.[^14]

## 8. Exclusions (Cases Not Covered)

Decree 03/2021/ND-CP lists specific cases where the insurer is exempt from paying indemnity; typical exclusions summarized in legal commentary include:[^18][^2]

- Damage intentionally caused by the motor vehicle owner, driver, or the victim.
- The at-fault driver intentionally fleeing the accident scene to avoid civil liability (with a limited exception where the driver flees but still fulfills civil liability obligations).
- The driver is underage or overage for the vehicle, or has no driving license, an invalid license, or a license not appropriate for the vehicle type, at the time of the accident.
- Indirect damage such as loss of commercial value or loss related to the use and operation of the damaged property (loss of use).
- Property damage occurring while the driver is under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or banned psychotropic substances.
- Damage to property arising from theft or robbery during or following the accident.
- Damage to special property such as gold, silver, precious stones, valuable papers (banknotes, securities), antiques, or valuable arts and photographs.
- Damage arising from war, terrorism, earthquakes, and other exceptional events specified by regulation.[^18][^3]

The compulsory insurance also does **not** cover damage to the insured motorcycle itself; own‑damage must be covered by voluntary policies if the owner wishes.[^10][^2]

## 9. Buying and Holding the Insurance

### 9.1 Where and how to buy

Compulsory motorcycle insurance can be purchased from any insurer licensed to provide compulsory civil liability insurance of motor vehicle users in Vietnam.[^2][^1]
Policies are sold through insurer branches, agents, garages, banks, online channels, and increasingly via mobile apps and electronic certificates.[^4][^10]

### 9.2 Information recorded on the certificate

The insurance certificate for motorcycles typically records:[^14][^1]

- Name and address of the motor vehicle user (policyholder) and, if different, the vehicle owner.
- Vehicle identification (license plate or temporary plate, frame and engine numbers, brand and model).
- Insurance period (start and end date and time).
- Liability limits for health/life and property as required by law.
- Name, address, and contact information of the insurer, including emergency hotline.

## 10. Claims and Compensation Procedures

### 10.1 Notification of accident

Insurance buyers or beneficiaries must notify the insurer of an accident **within 5 working days** from the date of occurrence, except in cases of force majeure.[^19][^14]
Notification may be made in writing or electronically (such as email or other forms accepted by the insurer), and must provide key information on time, place, vehicle, victims, and preliminary damage.[^14]

### 10.2 Advance payment by the insurer

Within **3 working days** after being notified of an accident, the insurer must make an **advance payment for bodily injury or death** in covered cases:[^5][^14]

- 70% of the statutory insurance claim per person per case in the event of death.
- 50% of the statutory insurance claim per person per case in the event of bodily injury requiring emergency treatment.

If it is not yet clear whether the accident falls within the scope of coverage, the insurer still has to advance part of the compensation (a lower percentage) according to Decree 03/2021/ND-CP to support victims while the investigation is ongoing.[^5][^14]

### 10.3 Time limit for lodging claims

Victims, insured persons, or their legal representatives must lodge a claim within **1 year from the date of the accident**, unless the delay is due to objective reasons or force majeure recognized by law.[^14]

### 10.4 Time limit for the insurer to settle claims

Once the insurer has received a **complete claim dossier**, it must settle and pay insurance compensation within **15 days**.
If verification is required (for example, awaiting police conclusions), the maximum time limit is **30 days** from the date of receipt of a complete dossier.[^19][^14]
If the insurer refuses to pay, it must provide a written explanation to the policyholder or beneficiary within **30 days** from the date of receiving the complete dossier.[^14]

### 10.5 Basis for determining compensation

Compensation for health and life damage is determined based on statutory schedules (Annex to the Decree) that assign compensation amounts or percentages of the maximum limit depending on the type and severity of injury, or according to agreement or court decision, but not exceeding the 150 million VND per person limit.[^15][^3]
Compensation for property damage to third parties is calculated based on actual, documented loss and the degree of fault of the motor vehicle user, capped at 50 million VND per accident for motorcycles.[^15][^3]

## 11. Motor Vehicle Insurance Fund (Humanitarian Support)

Vietnam maintains a **Motor Vehicle Insurance Fund** (MVIF) financed in part by contributions from insurers to provide humanitarian support in certain accidents.[^20][^3]
The Fund can support victims in cases where accidents are caused by uninsured, unknown, or excluded vehicles, by paying a portion of the statutory maximum coverage (for example, 30% of the maximum limit in case of death or very severe injury, 10% for certain lower levels of injury), subject to detailed conditions in Decree 67/2023/ND-CP.[^3]

## 12. Administrative Penalties for Motorcyclists

### 12.1 Current fine levels (from 2025 onwards)

Decree 168/2024/ND-CP, effective from January 1, 2025, increased fines for failure to carry or possess a valid compulsory civil liability insurance certificate for motorcycles.[^21][^8]
Under Clause 2 Article 18 of Decree 168/2024/ND-CP, drivers of motorcycles, mopeds and similar vehicles who **do not have** or **do not carry** a valid compulsory civil liability insurance certificate are subject to a fine of **200,000 to 300,000 VND**.[^8][^21]

### 12.2 Previous fine levels (before 2025)

Before Decree 168/2024/ND-CP came into force, Clause 2 Article 21 of Decree 100/2019/ND-CP, as amended by Decree 123/2021/ND-CP, set fines of **100,000 to 200,000 VND** for individuals operating motorcycles and similar vehicles without having or carrying a valid compulsory civil liability insurance certificate.[^9][^22][^8]

### 12.3 Other sanctions on insurers and entities

Decree 67/2023/ND-CP also stipulates administrative sanctions (monetary fines) for insurers and organizations that violate obligations related to compulsory insurance (such as failing to pay compensation on time or breaching product rules); depending on the violation, fines can be significant for organizations (for example, from tens of millions of VND for serious non‑compliance).[^23][^3]

## 13. Rights and Obligations of Parties

### 13.1 Obligations of motorcycle owners / motor vehicle users

Under Decree 03/2021/ND-CP, motorcycle owners and users must:[^1][^14]

- Purchase compulsory civil liability insurance before the vehicle participates in traffic, and maintain it continuously while the vehicle is used.
- Pay premiums fully and on time according to the insurance contract.
- Provide complete and truthful information about the vehicle and usage when buying insurance.
- Comply with traffic safety regulations and do not intentionally cause accidents.
- Notify the insurer promptly when an accident occurs and cooperate in investigation and claims handling.
- Preserve, within their ability, the accident scene and damaged property (unless required to act otherwise for rescue or safety reasons).

### 13.2 Rights of motorcycle owners / motor vehicle users

Motor vehicle users have rights including:[^20][^1][^14]

- To request the insurer to issue a proper insurance certificate and explain coverage terms and claims procedures.
- To receive advance payments and indemnities within the legal time limits when an insured accident occurs.
- To have insurance premiums included in business expenses (for enterprises) or recurrent expenses (for public entities) as allowed by law.
- To request the Motor Vehicle Insurance Fund to consider humanitarian support if conditions are met.
- To complain, request mediation or arbitration, or initiate lawsuits if they disagree with the insurer’s claim settlement.

### 13.3 Obligations of insurers

Insurers licensed to provide compulsory motorcycle insurance must:[^20][^1][^14]

- Design and file compulsory insurance products in accordance with Decree 03/2021/ND-CP, Decree 67/2023/ND-CP, and Circular 04/2021/TT-BTC.
- Issue insurance certificates with all required information immediately after concluding contracts and collecting premiums.
- Maintain hotlines and systems for receiving accident notifications 24/7 and respond promptly.
- Conduct loss assessment, coordinate with authorities, and advance payments within 3 working days for bodily injury and death.
- Settle legitimate claims fully and on time (15 days/30 days) and provide written reasons for any refusal.
- Contribute to and properly use the Motor Vehicle Insurance Fund according to law.
- Report annual premium income and claim ratios for compulsory insurance to the Ministry of Finance and other regulators.[^11][^20]

## 14. Practical Notes for Motorcycle Owners

Although the statutory premium for small motorcycles is low (55,000–60,000 VND per year excluding VAT), the insurance provides substantial protection: up to 150 million VND per victim for health and life and 50 million VND per accident for property damage caused by the motorcycle.[^16][^6][^7]
Failure to buy or carry valid compulsory insurance exposes drivers to administrative fines and may leave victims without timely indemnity from insurers or the Motor Vehicle Insurance Fund, increasing the financial burden on the at‑fault rider and their family.[^8][^16]
Buying and keeping a valid compulsory motorcycle insurance policy, together with respecting traffic laws, is therefore both a legal obligation and an important risk‑management measure for road users in Vietnam.[^16][^10]
